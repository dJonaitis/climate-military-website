The Climate Crisis

There have been 28 United Nations Climate Change Conferences (COP) as of May, 2024. Emissions goals were set for 38 developed countries since the Kyoto Protocol in 1997. 36 remained after the United States did not ratify and Canada withdrew.

At the turn of the millenium, 3 years had passed since these goals were first outlined. The Paris Agreements, agreed to in 2015 at COP 21 set out to slash emissions by 50% until 2030. The science says that is a necessity if we want to stay below 1.5 Â°C above pre-industrial levels. That is a significant tipping point, beyond which our climate, marine and terrestrial ecosystems and human health take a significant toll.

Has that materialised?
